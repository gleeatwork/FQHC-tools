Your population — FQHC Asian-specific guidance
Chinese & Korean patients — BMI thresholds Standard BMI cutoffs underestimate metabolic risk. Use WHO Asia-Pacific criteria: ≥23 kg/m² = overweight, ≥27.5 kg/m² = obese. A patient with BMI 25 who appears "normal" by US standards is overweight by Asian criteria. Screen earlier for metabolic syndrome, prediabetes, and dyslipidemia.
Vietnamese patients — TG-dominant pattern Higher prevalence of hypertriglyceridemia and low HDL. Non-HDL-C (= TC − HDL) is often more informative than LDL-C alone — calculate it at every visit. NAFLD/MASLD is common and independently raises CVD risk beyond PREVENT inputs. Check LFTs and TG.
South Asian patients — formal risk enhancer (2026 guideline) South Asian ancestry is now a named risk enhancer in the 2026 ACC/AHA guideline. Treat borderline risk (3–5%) as intermediate. Check Lp(a) — elevated in ~35% of South Asians regardless of LDL-C.
All Asian patients — statin pharmacogenomics CYP2C9 / SLCO1B1 variants are more prevalent in East Asian populations → higher plasma exposure with simvastatin and lovastatin → increased myopathy risk.
  • Prefer rosuvastatin (not CYP2C9-dependent; less interaction)
  • FDA cap: simvastatin ≤20 mg in Asian patients
  • Rosuvastatin 5–10 mg achieves moderate-intensity LDL reduction in most Asian patients (often equivalent to 20–40 mg in non-Asian patients)
  • Pravastatin is a safe alternative if rosuvastatin not tolerated
Medi-Cal / formulary notes Rosuvastatin: covered on Medi-Cal (generic available). Ezetimibe: covered generic. PCSK9 inhibitors: require PA on Medi-Cal; document failure of max-dose statin + ezetimibe. Bempedoic acid: check current formulary — may require step therapy documentation.

When PREVENT may underestimate risk in your patients: Family history of premature ASCVD · Lp(a) ≥50 mg/dL · NAFLD/MASLD · Sleep apnea · Chronic steroid use · HIV/ART therapy · hsCRP ≥2.0 · Premature menopause

For any of these: upgrade clinical concern regardless of PREVENT score. CAC is the best reclassification tool when the score alone seems too low for the clinical picture.
